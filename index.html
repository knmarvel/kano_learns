Here are a few of my favorite things on the internet

  • Six Degrees of Kevin Bacon

    I love discovery! One of my favorite games to play is Six Degrees of Kevin Bacon on Wikipedia. The rules are:

    1. Go to a random article on Wikipedia.
    2. Find a link on the page is most likely to have information about Kevin Bacon.
    3. Find a link on this new page that gets you even closer to information about Kevin Bacon
    4. Repeat until a link you click is the Wikipedia page about Kevin Bacon. If you've gotten there in six or fewer clicks, you win the game!
    5. You personally win regardless because you get to find out new information with each Wikipedia page, making you a more well-rounded person. Yay, you!
  • Ready for this throwback to 2004? Can't live without Homestar Runner! You can still find the old site, but as it runs on Flash, YouTube is more accesssible for Apple users.
  • DuckDuckGo is a search engine that claims it doesn't sell or store your data. You still get ads, but not the same ads on every page. For instance, I searched for professional clothing with DuckDuckGo, and lingered a few moments on an awesome shirt. With Google, I would see that shirt on every page with Google ads for the next week. With DuckDuckGo, I don't see ads for that shirt anywhere.
  • I am a mythical beast.

About

I've been in the broad field of office administration since 2012. I worked in a college financial aid office, as the office administrator of an aerospace corporation, and as business manager/interim director of a library. I realized a few things about my current career arc:

  1. I need a masters degree to move up as a librarian, which would cost up to 4 years salary of a highly contested library director job that I have no guarantee of attaining.
  2. If I wanted to pursue business administration, again, highly contested field with low pay.
  3. If I wanted to specialize to get higher pay, I'd need a degree in accounting or business, which means a master's degree and then doing the job of solving the same types of problems over and over again.
  4. With my current skills, I am limited by my location. I need the ability to work remotely.

So I decided to do something completely different than what I had been doing before. I have spent over 200 hours of my life playing Human Resource Machine and its sequel, 7 Billion Humans. I came across a few reviews from software engineers that said, "It seems silly to play a game exactly like my job," and I thought: "Oh, that's it. I could program for a living."

In July of 2019, I embarked on a year of learning and creative exploration. One large part of that is Kenzie Academy, a software engineering program that is more than a bootcamp. It's like a trade school for web design and software engineering, complete with an apprenticeship, that awards a certificate from Butler University and has a vested interest in ensuring that my skills make me highly employable. Another part of that is encouraging myself to have artistic freedom and to share my creations.
